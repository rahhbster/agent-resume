design, refine, document, and maintain it. This page is our way of saying thank
you — and a durable record of who built what.

## Maintainers

Maintainers own the direction of the standard, review proposals, and have merge
rights. They are responsible for keeping the schemas coherent and the versioning
policy honored.

## How to get listed

We add people here as they contribute — you don't need to ask, but you're
welcome to.

- **Contributors:** once your first meaningful PR is merged, a maintainer adds
  you to the **Contributors** list (or add yourself in the same PR — see below).
- **Core contributors:** promoted after sustained, significant contributions.
- **Maintainers:** invited by existing maintainers based on a track record of
  trusted, high-quality work and good stewardship of the standard.

### Adding yourself in your PR

When you open a PR, feel free to add a line to the **Contributors** section in
the same PR:

```markdown
- [@your-handle](https://github.com/your-handle) — what you changed (#PR)
```

Keep it to one concise line. Maintainers may tidy formatting on merge.

### What counts as "significant"

Judgment, not a checklist — but roughly: a merged change that improves the
standard or its tooling for everyone. Typos and trivial tweaks are appreciated
but generally rolled up rather than individually listed; a meaningful schema
fix, a new adapter, a language binding, a substantive doc/spec improvement, or a
well-scoped feature all qualify.

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](./CONTRIBUTING.md) for how to propose changes.
